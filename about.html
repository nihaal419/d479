About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical Pacific island with varied terrain, beautiful beaches, rainforests, and a small active volcano. The island has about 20,000 residents and a growing tourism industry.

Culture & Practical Information

Taniti uses U.S. dollars, has 120-volt power, and English is spoken mainly by younger residents. Alcohol sales are restricted at night, crime is rare, and many businesses close for national holidays.

FAQs

What currency is used on Taniti? ˅
Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and most major credit cards are accepted.
Is English widely spoken on Taniti? ˅
Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, but very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.
Are there any special laws or customs visitors should know? ˅
Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age is 18 but not strictly enforced. Many businesses close for national holidays.
Is Taniti a safe place to visit? ˅
Violent crime is very rare, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Always take normal precautions with valuables.
